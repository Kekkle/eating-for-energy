## Energy Basics

### BQ1. (True or False, Difficulty: 1)

**Statement:** Our brains need a constant supply of energy for thinking, studying, and reading.

**Answer:** True ✅

**Explanation:** Our brains need a constant supply of energy. We also need energy in our muscles for movement and even to digest our food.

### BQ2. (Multiple Choice, Difficulty: 1)

**Question:** Why are carbohydrates, protein, and fat called "macronutrients"?

- A) Because they are the most expensive nutrients
- B) Because we consume them in the largest quantities ✅
- C) Because they are only found in large foods
- D) Because they were discovered first

**Explanation:** Macro means large -- these are called macronutrients because we consume them in the largest quantities compared to other nutrients.

## Simple vs Complex Carbs

### BQ3. (Multiple Choice, Difficulty: 2)

**Question:** Which of the following is an example of a refined carbohydrate that is digested quickly?

- A) Brown rice
- B) Sweet potato
- C) White bread ✅
- D) Wholemeal pasta

**Explanation:** White bread is a refined carbohydrate that is digested and absorbed quickly, causing a sharp rise in blood glucose. Brown rice, sweet potato, and wholemeal pasta are complex carbohydrates that release energy more slowly.

---

### BQ4. (Multiple Choice, Difficulty: 2)

**Question:** What effect do complex carbohydrates (like brown rice and wholemeal bread) have compared to refined carbs?

- A) They are absorbed even faster than refined carbs
- B) They take longer to digest, releasing glucose more slowly ✅
- C) They contain no glucose at all
- D) They have exactly the same effect as refined carbs

**Explanation:** Complex carbohydrates take the body longer to digest and break down into glucose, ensuring a slower release into the blood and more stable blood glucose levels.

## Hidden Sugars and Food Labels

### BQ5. (True or False, Difficulty: 2)

**Statement:** Some foods that seem healthy, like cereal bars and fruit yogurts, can actually contain high levels of hidden sugar.

**Answer:** True ✅

**Explanation:** Even foods that most people expect to be healthy can contain high levels of "free" sugar. Cereal bars, fruit yogurts, and even pasta sauces can be surprisingly high in sugar.

---

### BQ6. (Multiple Choice, Difficulty: 2)

**Question:** Which of the following is NOT another name for sugar on a food label?

- A) Dextrose
- B) Maltodextrin
- C) Molasses
- D) Calcium ✅

**Explanation:** Sugar can hide under many names on food labels including dextrose, glucose, fructose, maltose, sucrose, syrup, maltodextrin, and molasses. Calcium is a mineral, not a sugar.

### BQ7. (Multiple Choice, Difficulty: 3)

**Question:** When reading a food label, how many grams of sugar per 100g is considered "high in sugar"?

- A) 2g per 100g
- B) 5g per 100g
- C) 10g per 100g ✅
- D) 20g per 100g

**Explanation:** As a rough guide, 10g of sugar per 100g is considered high in sugar, while 2g per 100g is considered low. One teaspoon of sugar equals about 4g.

### BQ8. (Multiple Choice, Difficulty: 2)

**Question:** How many teaspoons of sugar are in one 330ml can of Coke?

- A) 3 teaspoons
- B) 5 teaspoons
- C) 9 teaspoons ✅
- D) 15 teaspoons

**Explanation:** One 330ml can of Coke contains about 35g or 9 teaspoons of sugar -- that's more than the whole day's recommended intake of about 30g in just one drink!

### BQ9. (True or False, Difficulty: 2)

**Statement:** No more than 5% of our daily energy should come from sugars, which equals about 30g.

**Answer:** True ✅

**Explanation:** The recommendation is that no more than 5% of daily energy intake should come from added sugars, which works out to roughly 30g per day.

## Protein

### BQ10. (Multiple Choice, Difficulty: 1)

**Question:** Protein is vital for which of the following?

- A) Only building muscles
- B) Growth and repair of tissues, making enzymes, and supporting the immune system ✅
- C) Only providing quick energy
- D) Only healthy skin

**Explanation:** Protein forms the building blocks for muscles and tissues, enzymes for digestion, and antibodies for our immune system. It's vital for growth and repair, and is also important for healthy hair and skin.

---

### BQ11. (True or False, Difficulty: 1)

**Statement:** Including protein with carbohydrates helps slow down digestion and supports better blood glucose balance.

**Answer:** True ✅

**Explanation:** Protein is digested more slowly than carbohydrate. When eaten together, the meal is digested more slowly than carbohydrate alone, supporting better blood glucose balance and steadier energy.

## Fats

### BQ12. (Multiple Choice, Difficulty: 2)

**Question:** Which of the following foods is a source of healthy "essential" fats?

- A) White bread
- B) Salmon ✅
- C) Breakfast cereal
- D) Biscuits

**Explanation:** Essential fats are found in foods like nuts, seeds, salmon, mackerel, avocados, and olive oil. They support brain function and mental health, among many other benefits.

---

### BQ13. (True or False, Difficulty: 2)

**Statement:** Healthy fats are important for brain function and mental health.

**Answer:** True ✅

**Explanation:** Some fats known as "essential fats" have several health benefits including supporting brain function and mental health. They are found in nuts, seeds, oily fish, avocados, and olive oil.

## Vitamins, Minerals, and the Rainbow

**Question:** Why is it good to eat fruits and vegetables of many different colours?

- A) It makes your plate look nicer
- B) Different colours provide different vitamins and minerals ✅
- C) Colourful foods always taste better
- D) It doesn't matter what colour they are

**Statement:** Vitamins and minerals are needed to turn fuel from food into energy. Without them, the process doesn't work properly.

**Question:** What is a well-balanced breakfast?

- A) Just toast on its own
- B) A complex carbohydrate combined with protein and a healthy fat ✅
- C) Only a glass of fruit juice

## Snacking

### BQ17. (True or False, Difficulty: 1)

**Statement:** If you fancy something sweet like chocolate, eating it directly after a well-balanced meal can help avoid blood sugar highs and lows.

**Answer:** True ✅

**Explanation:** Eating something sweet after a balanced meal, when your stomach is full, slows the absorption of glucose into your blood and helps avoid the blood sugar roller coaster.

## Food Myths and Fads

### BQ18. (True or False, Difficulty: 2)

**Statement:** Fad diets that exclude a complete food group can make you tired and irritable because you miss out on important nutrients.

**Answer:** True ✅

**Explanation:** Fad diets can limit what you eat or exclude entire food groups, which means missing out on important nutrients. This can leave you tired and irritable. A healthy, balanced, and varied diet is a better approach.

## Drinks

### BQ19. (Multiple Choice, Difficulty: 2)

**Question:** Why might "zero sugar" or "diet" drinks still not be a healthy choice?

- A) They contain even more sugar than regular drinks
- B) They may contain artificial sweeteners which may not be good for health ✅
- C) They have no flavour
- D) They are more expensive

**Explanation:** "No added sugar", "zero sugar", and "sugar-free" drinks usually contain little or no sugar, but may contain artificial sweeteners like Saccharine, Aspartame, and Acesulfame which may not be good for our health.

---

### BQ20. (Multiple Choice, Difficulty: 1)

**Question:** About what percentage of our body is made of water?

- A) 20%
- B) 40%
- C) 60% ✅
- D) 80%

**Explanation:** About 60% of our body is made of water. We lose water through sweat, especially during exercise and hot weather. Aim for 6-8 glasses or 1.2 litres of water every day.
