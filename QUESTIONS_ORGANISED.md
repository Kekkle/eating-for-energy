## Topic 1: Macronutrients & Balanced Eating (13 questions)

_What are macronutrients, what do they do, and how do we eat well?_

---

### 1.1 (MC, Difficulty: 1)

**Question:** Which macronutrient is the body's preferred source of energy?

- A) Protein
- B) Carbohydrates ✅
- C) Fat
- D) Vitamins

**Explanation:** Carbohydrates are broken down into glucose which fuels every cell, especially the brain.

---

### 1.2 (MC, Difficulty: 1)

**Question:** Why are carbohydrates, protein, and fat called "macronutrients"?

- A) Because they are the most expensive nutrients
- B) Because we consume them in the largest quantities ✅
- C) Because they are only found in large foods
- D) Because they were discovered first

**Explanation:** Macro means large! Macronutrients are nutrients we consume in the largest quantities compared to other nutrients.

### 1.4 (T/F, Difficulty: 1)

**Statement:** Fats are a quicker energy source than carbohydrates.

**Answer:** False ✅

**Explanation:** Carbohydrates are used preferentially over protein and fats by the body for energy, especially for the brain.

---

### 1.5 (MC, Difficulty: 2)

**Question:** Which macronutrient is especially important for fuelling the brain?

- A) Fat
- B) Protein
- C) Carbohydrates ✅
- D) Fibre

**Explanation:** Carbohydrates provide the main energy source particularly for the brain.

---

### 1.6 (MC, Difficulty: 1)

**Question:** A balanced meal should include which food groups?

- A) Only carbohydrates
- B) Protein and carbohydrates only
- C) Protein, carbohydrates, and fats ✅
- D) Only protein and fats

**Explanation:** Aiming for 80/20 approach means eating whole foods (veggies, non-processed meats, whole grains) about 80% of the time.

---


### 1.8 (MC, Difficulty: 2)

**Question:** Why is it a good idea to eat a source of protein with every meal?

- A) Protein tastes better than other foods
- B) Protein takes longer to break down, giving gradual energy release ✅
- C) Protein has more calories
- D) Protein only helps with sleep

**Explanation:** Protein digests more slowly than carbohydrates, providing gradual energy release and satiety (feeling full).

---

### 1.9 (Multi-Select, Difficulty: 1)

- B) Growth and repair of tissues ✅

**Explanation:** Protein forms the building blocks for muscles and tissues, enzymes for digestion, and antibodies for our immune system. It's vital for growth and repair, and is also important for healthy hair and skin.

## Topic 2: Blood Sugar & Energy (9 questions)

_How does blood sugar work, what controls it, and how does it affect our energy?_

---

### 2.1 (MC, Difficulty: 1)

**Question:** When we eat carbohydrates, they are broken down into which sugar?

- A) Fructose
- B) Sucrose
- C) Glucose ✅
- D) Lactose

**Explanation:** Carbohydrates are broken down into glucose, which is used by the cells of the body to make energy.

---

### 2.2 (MC, Difficulty: 2)

**Question:** Too much glucose in the bloodstream is _______ to the body.

**Select the correct word:**
- A) Energizing
- B) Toxic ✅
- C) Sleep-promoting

**Explanation:** Yes! That's why blood sugar levels are tightly regulated by the hormone insulin.

---

### 2.3 (MC, Difficulty: 1)

**Question:** Which hormone regulates blood sugar levels?

- A) Adrenaline
- B) Insulin ✅
- C) Cortisol
- D) Serotonin

**Explanation:** Insulin is released from the pancreas to bring blood sugar levels within normal limits after eating.

---

### 2.4 (MC, Difficulty: 2)

**Question:** Where is excess glucose stored in the body?

- A) Only in the brain
- B) In liver and muscle cells, and the extra in fat cells ✅
- C) Only in the stomach
- D) It is immediately used up

**Explanation:** Glucose is stored in liver and muscle cells. When there's too much, the excess is stored in adipose (fat) cells.

---

### 2.5 (T/F, Difficulty: 2)

**Statement:** No more than 5% of our daily energy intake should come from sugars.

**Answer:** True ✅

**Explanation:** The recommendation is that no more than 5% of daily energy intake should come from added sugars, which works out to roughly 30g per day.

---

### 2.6 (T/F, Difficulty: 2)

**Statement:** Dehydration can impact blood sugar regulation.

**Answer:** True ✅

**Explanation:** Reduced water in the bloodstream increases the concentration of glucose. Drinking water consistently helps to keep blood sugar in a healthy range.

---


### 3.3 (MC, Difficulty: 2) — Hidden Sugars

**Question:** Which of the following is NOT another name for sugar on a food label?

- A) Dextrose
- B) Maltodextrin
- C) Molasses
- D) Calcium ✅

**Explanation:** Sugar can hide under many names on food labels including dextrose, glucose, fructose, maltose, sucrose, syrup, maltodextrin, and molasses. Calcium is a mineral, not a sugar.

## Notes for Future Activity Types

### Drag-and-Drop Ideas

- Sort foods into macronutrient categories (carbs / protein / fats)
- Sort carbs into "whole grain" vs "refined"

### Create-It Ideas

- Build a balanced breakfast plate (drag carbs--starchy carbs plus fibre-rich carbs), protein, and fats onto a plate)
- Create a lower-sugar drink (drag ingredients into a cup/bottle)

### Order-It Ideas

- Put the blood sugar regulation process in order (eat carbs → broken down to glucose → blood sugar rises → insulin released → glucose stored → blood sugar returns to normal)
- Put the "blood sugar roller coaster" cycle in order

### Matching Pairs Ideas

- Match foods to their macronutrient group

### Scenario Ideas

- "Amara has a football match at 2pm. It's now 11am. What should she eat?"
- "Kofi feels tired and can't concentrate in class after lunch. What might help?"
- "Priya is about to go for a run. Should she have a sports drink before or after warming up?"
