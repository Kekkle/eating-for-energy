## Topic: Energy & Macronutrients (8 questions)

### E1. (Multiple Choice, Difficulty: 1)

**Question:** Which macronutrient is the body's preferred source of energy?

- A) Protein
- B) Carbohydrates ✅
- C) Fat
- D) Vitamins

**Explanation:** Carbohydrates are broken down into glucose which fuels every cell, especially the brain.

---

### BQ2. (Multiple Choice, Difficulty: 1)

**Question:** Why are carbohydrates, protein, and fat called "macronutrients"?

- A) Because they are the most expensive nutrients
- B) Because we consume them in the largest quantities ✅
- C) Because they are only found in large foods
- D) Because they were discovered first

**Explanation:** Macro means large! Macronutrients are nutrients we consume in the large quantities compared to other nutrients.

---

### E2. (True or False, Difficulty: 1)

**Statement:** Every cell in our body requires energy to function.

**Answer:** True ✅

**Explanation:** That's right! Energy is necessary for every body process — without it, we cannot survive.

---

### E4. (True or False, Difficulty: 1)

**Statement:** Fats are a quicker energy source than carbohydrates.

**Answer:** False ✅

**Explanation:** Carbohydrates are used preferentially over protein and fats by the body for energy, especially for the brain.

---

### E5. (Multiple Choice, Difficulty: 2)

**Question:** Which macronutrient is especially important for fuelling the brain?

- A) Fat
- B) Protein
- C) Carbohydrates ✅
- D) Fibre

**Explanation:** Carbohydrates provide the main energy source particularly for the brain.

---

### E6. (Multiple Choice, Difficulty: 1)

**Question:** A balanced meal should include which food groups?

- A) Only carbohydrates
- B) Protein and carbohydrates only
- C) Protein, carbohydrates, and fats ✅
- D) Only protein and fats

## Topic: Blood Sugar (9 questions)

### BS1. (Multiple Choice, Difficulty: 1)

**Question:** When we eat carbohydrates, they are broken down into which sugar?

- A) Fructose
- B) Sucrose
- C) Glucose ✅
- D) Lactose

**Explanation:** Carbohydrates are broken down into glucose, which is used by the cells of the body to make energy.

---

### BS2. (True or False, Difficulty: 2)

**Statement:** Too much glucose in the bloodstream is _______ to the body.

**Select the correct word:** 
- A) Energizing
- B) Toxic ✅
- C) Sleep-promoting

**Explanation:** Yes! That's why blood sugar levels are tightly regulated by the hormone insulin.

---

### BS3. (Multiple Choice, Difficulty: 1)

**Question:** Which hormone regulates blood sugar levels?

- A) Adrenaline
- B) Insulin ✅
- C) Cortisol
- D) Serotonin

**Explanation:** Insulin is released from the pancreas to bring blood sugar levels within normal limits after eating.

---

### BS5. (True or False, Difficulty: 2)

**Statement:** After a sugary meal, blood sugar rises _______.

**Select the correct word:** 
- A) Quickly ✅
- B) Slowly 

**Explanation:** After a sugary meal or one heavy in carbs, blood sugar can rise too rapidly. This causes too much insulin to be released, leading to a blood sugar dip.

---

### BS6. (Multiple Choice, Difficulty: 2)

**Question:** What happens when too much insulin is released after a sugar spike?

- A) You feel energised for hours
- B) Blood sugar stays stable
- C) You experience a blood sugar dip with low energy and cravings ✅
- D) Your muscles grow stronger

**Explanation:** Too much insulin causes a blood sugar dip — this is where we feel low energy, mood changes, and cravings for sugary foods. This cycle is the "blood sugar roller coaster".

---

### BS8. (Multiple Choice, Difficulty: 2)

**Question:** Where is excess glucose stored in the body?

- A) Only in the brain
- B) In liver and muscle cells, and the extra in fat cells ✅
- C) Only in the stomach
- D) It is immediately used up

**Explanation:** Glucose is stored in liver and muscle cells. When there's too much, the excess is stored in adipose (fat) cells.

## Simple vs Complex Carbs

### BQ3. (Multiple Choice, Difficulty: 2)

**Question:** Sort the following foods between refined carbohydrates (simple carbs) and complex carbohydrates: 

**Drag and Drop:** 
Simple carbs (use an image for each food)
- White bread ✅ 
- Sugar cereal ✅
- Apple juice ✅
- Donut ✅

Complex carbs:
- Brown bread ✅
- Potato ✅
- Broccoli ✅
- Banana ✅

**Explanation:** Refined carbohydrates are digested and absorbed quickly, causing a sharp rise in blood glucose. Complex carbohydrates take longer to digest, releasing glucose more slowly.

### BQ6. (Multiple Choice, Difficulty: 2)

**Question:** Which of the following is NOT another name for sugar on a food label?

- A) Dextrose
- B) Maltodextrin
- C) Molasses
- D) Calcium ✅

**Explanation:** Sugar can hide under many names on food labels including dextrose, glucose, fructose, maltose, sucrose, syrup, maltodextrin, and molasses. Calcium is a mineral, not a sugar.

### BQ7. (Multiple Choice, Difficulty: 3)

- A) 10g per 100g ✅
- B) 5g per 100g
- C) 2g per 100g
- D) 20g per 100g

**Explanation:** As a rough guide, 10g of sugar per 100g is considered high in sugar, while 2g per 100g is considered low. One teaspoon of sugar equals about 4g.

## Protein

### BQ10. (Multiple Choice, Difficulty: 1)

**Question:** Protein is vital for which of the following?

**Select all that apply:**
- A) Building muscles ✅
- B) Growth and repair of tissues ✅
- C) Providing quick energy
- D) Making enzymes ✅
- E) Supporting the immune system  ✅
- F) Improving hydration levels

**Explanation:** Protein forms the building blocks for muscles and tissues, enzymes for digestion, and antibodies for our immune system. It's vital for growth and repair, and is also important for healthy hair and skin.

---

### BQ11. (True or False, Difficulty: 1)

**Statement:** Including protein with carbohydrates hinders digestion and raises blood glucose quickly.

**Answer:** True ✅

**Explanation:** Protein is digested more slowly than carbohydrate. When eaten together, the meal is digested more slowly than carbohydrate alone, supporting better blood glucose balance and steadier energy.
